About Digital Blackfriars

Here is a link to try out the panorama! There is still a lot of tweaking left to do. This research project is still ongoing.

As part of my Undergraduate Research Project, I performed the role of a concept artist and provided concept sketches for Digitial Blackfriars. I provided sketches for hand-drawn panoramas and designed a project logo. Below is an abstract I wrote that describes my research:

"Ever wonder what it felt like walking through the streets of Blackfriars, London during the mid-1500s and early 1600s? In our day and age, it would be second nature to simply google search the location and time period; however, there is very little information (specifically imagery) of Blackfriars during this time period -- a period when Shakespeare and other playwrights had companies there. So much information has been lost. That being said, there is still a lot of text from this time. This research projects seeks to transform this text into data, and use that data to visually communicate what it meant to be in Blackfriars, London during this time period. The method used to visually communicate this text and data is through the use of augmented reality. By using an app, “Argon”, hand-drawn panoramas can be rendered and viewed through the computer screen and through an Apple device as if you were physically present in that space. Understanding what the architecture looked like at the time, and understanding how to draw panoramas by hand is the largest challenge. With further architectural information, the project will be very close to implementation and users will be able to experience Blackfriars, London for themselves. The benefits of this research in hand-drawn panoramas doesn’t only apply to this project, but it also extends to different disciplines such as architecture, urban planning, film, and games."
