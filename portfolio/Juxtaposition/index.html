Juxtaposition

About Juxtaposition

"Juxtaposition" is a work that I made for one of my Animation 1 projects. This was a fun project to work on because I was able to use the photographs I took while I was on vacation in Vancouver and Alaska as my reference images. At first I found a picture of a glacier that I really liked (it was about to break). Then I found a picture of a really pristine looking body of water. Then I found a mountain that had really interesting textures in it. So I decided to model all three of the environments in one and form this really odd juxtaposition of seasons. It was fun thinking of how the stream separates two different worlds where one is nice and beautiful, and the other one is harsh and cold. The project did not involve any criteria for adding materials to the items, however, I decided the essence of the image wouldn’t be complete without application of said materials. Hence, I went above and beyond and applied the material to what was a very high poly mesh and significantly upped my render time. However, in the end, I’d say it was quite worth it.
